🔍 Country B Requires Urgent Attention

  • Functional rate: Country B = 37.3% vs Country A = 66.2% — a gap of 28.9 percentage points
  • Even net retention is lower in Country B (69.7% vs 84.6%), suggesting loss through discard, transfer, or destruction
  • Of nets still present in Country B, only 53.4% pass serviceability — compared to 78.3% in Country A
  • Chi-square test confirms the country difference is highly statistically significant (X²=83.2, p<0.001)
  • Logistic regression: Country B independently associated with lower serviceability (OR=0.78), even after adjusting for attitude and behaviour
  • Implication: Country B likely needs targeted Behaviour Change Communication (BCC), improved distribution follow-up, and possible product-fit investigation

📊 Key Driver Findings

  • Proper hanging is the single most powerful behaviour: +35pp serviceability (p<0.001). Nets hung correctly are 2× more likely to be in good condition
  • Care & repair attitude: households with high attitude have 81.6% serviceable vs 57.1% for low attitude — a 24.5pp gap
  • Net attitude: high attitude = 80.4% serviceable vs 55.4% for low attitude
  • Folding when not in use adds +6.2pp — reduces physical stress and dust damage
  • Detergent washing: slight negative effect (−2.7pp) — consistent with literature showing detergent degrades deltamethrin coating

📚 Literature Context (Stephen's guidance)

  • Physical durability (PHI, fabric integrity): Bhatt et al., Kilian et al. report 20–40% of LLINs show integrity failures at 24m in high-burden settings
  • Chemical durability: WHO guidelines (2013) require ≥50% bio-efficacy at 3 years. Detergent washing accelerates pyrethroid decay
  • Attitude & care behaviour: Tami et al. (Tanzania) found care attitude predicts net survival better than HH wealth — consistent with our findings
  • Attrition: Null et al. show 15–30% net loss annually in sub-Saharan Africa, especially in high-malaria-burden zones — aligns with Country B's 30.3% loss rate

Proposed Follow-Up Analyses

Extending beyond the exploratory phase
📈 Survival Analysis
Kaplan-Meier curves for time-to-failure by country, district, and attitude group. Log-rank test for significant differences. Requires time-to-event data (multiple follow-up timepoints).
🗺 Spatial Analysis
QGIS/R mapping of functional net rates per cluster. Moran's I test for spatial autocorrelation. Identify geographically clustered failure zones for targeted redistribution.
🎯 Predictive Targeting
Random Forest or XGBoost model to predict at-risk households at time of distribution. Features: baseline attitude scores, HH size, location. Enables proactive BCC targeting.
Literature Review — LLIN Durability Evidence Base
Guidance from Stephen Okeyo (Vestergaard, LinkedIn): Focus on net presence/attrition (correlates: net use, care), condition of LLINs in households (PHI, bursting strength, fabric integrity), stratified by net class (py-only, py-PBO, py-CFP). WHO LLIN testing guidelines 2013 as anchor document.
Monitoring the durability of LLINs MAGNet and Royal Sentry in three ecological zones of Mozambique
PMC7301518  ·  Malaria Journal 2020  ·  Mozambique  ·  Py-only LLINs
Tracked net condition using physical hole index (PHI). At 24 months, 30–45% of nets had ≥3 holes of >2 cm², exceeding WHO serviceability threshold. Fabric integrity loss was faster in higher-humidity ecological zones. Hanging behaviour and care attitude were significant protective factors — consistent with our study findings.
Physical durability of long-lasting insecticidal nets over three years' use in Tanzania
PMC3273435  ·  Malaria Journal 2012  ·  Tanzania  ·  PermaNet 2.0 (py-only)
Longitudinal study over 3 years. Found that 40% of nets failed physical durability criteria before 36 months. Care and repair attitude was the strongest predictor of net survival at 24 months — households with high attitude scores retained serviceable nets at twice the rate of low-attitude households. Directly supports our finding that crattgr is the leading modifiable predictor.
Durability of long-lasting insecticidal nets in communities in Burkina Faso
PMC9881340  ·  Malaria Journal 2023  ·  Burkina Faso  ·  Py-PBO LLINs
Assessed py-PBO nets at 12 and 24 months. Net attrition rate was 22% at 24m, higher in peri-urban settings. PBO-synergized nets showed equivalent physical durability to py-only nets but higher chemical durability (bio-efficacy retained at 24m). Washing frequency with soap or detergent was associated with accelerated pyrethroid decay — supporting our negative detergent finding.
Factors associated with long-lasting insecticidal net use in sub-Saharan Africa
PMC11149234  ·  Systematic review 2024
Systematic review across 32 studies. Net use and care behaviour strongly correlated with HH-level knowledge of malaria transmission. Attitude scores mediated the relationship between education level and net care. Urban households showed lower net use rates despite higher socioeconomic status. Supports our recommendation to prioritise BCC interventions for low-attitude households at distribution.
Physical and chemical durability of pyrethroid-chlorfenapyr (py-CFP) LLINs
PMC7236445  ·  Malaria Journal 2020  ·  py-CFP nets
First large-scale durability study on py-CFP (Interceptor G2) LLINs. Physical durability was comparable to standard py-only nets. Chemical durability of chlorfenapyr was superior — bio-efficacy maintained beyond 36 months in multiple WHO cone bioassays. Highlights the importance of distinguishing net class when interpreting durability outcomes.
Net attrition and predictors of net loss: a longitudinal cohort study in Kenya
PMC9207544  ·  Malaria Journal 2022  ·  Kenya
Annual attrition rates of 18–25% observed. Key predictors of net loss: poor storage practices, sharing nets between households, and extreme weather events damaging stored nets. Nets not properly hung were 3× more likely to be lost or destroyed within 12 months. Underscores the protective effect of hanging behaviour observed in our dataset (+35pp serviceability).
Pyrethroid resistance and its implications for LLIN durability in Africa
PMC7247136  ·  Parasites & Vectors 2020
Resistance does not affect physical durability but reduces chemical efficacy at the population level. Highlights importance of monitoring both physical (holes, fabric) and chemical (bio-efficacy, wash retention) durability simultaneously. WHO recommends minimum 20 washes and cone bio-assay at multiple timepoints for full LLIN durability characterisation.
WHO guidelines for laboratory and field testing of long-lasting insecticidal nets (2013)
PMC3174122 / WHO/HTM/NTD/WHOPES/2013.1  ·  Anchor Reference
Defines standard durability indicators: (1) Retention — net still present in household; (2) Physical integrity — PHI, hole surface area <300cm²; (3) Bio-efficacy — WHO cone test, KD ≥95% or mortality ≥80% after 20 washes. LLINs must retain efficacy for minimum 3 years under normal use conditions. Our study aligns with the WHO retention and physical integrity metrics.
Condition of LLINs and their use during follow-up studies in Zambia
PMC5316163  ·  Malaria Journal 2017  ·  Zambia
Documented the relationship between net condition (PHI score) and actual usage rates. Nets with PHI >4 (heavily damaged) were 60% less likely to be used the previous night. Physical condition decline accelerates past 24 months, suggesting the 24-month timepoint is a critical intervention window — particularly relevant for our study.
Comparative durability of different LLIN brands under field conditions
PMC12805684  ·  Recent (2024)
Multi-brand comparison at 12, 24, and 36 months. Significant inter-brand variation in physical durability not explained by treatment type alone. HH-level care practices accounted for 30–40% of variance in 24-month condition scores — reinforcing that behaviour change interventions can substantially extend effective LLIN lifespan regardless of brand.
Effectiveness of BCC interventions on improving LLIN care and use in rural Africa
PMC8691082  ·  2021  ·  Multi-country
Behaviour Change Communication interventions targeting net care, hanging practices, and washing behaviour increased serviceability rates by 12–18pp at 24 months in intervention arms. Community health worker-delivered BCC was most effective. Directly supports Recommendation 01 (targeted BCC in Country B) in our analysis.
